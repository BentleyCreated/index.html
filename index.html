The Gold Standard in Data Protection

The Benefits of Encryption

  • Privacy: Keeps your data private and inaccessible to unauthorized users.
  • Security: Protects your data from breaches and theft.
  • Integrity: Helps protect the integrity of your data by preventing unauthorized alterations.
  • Authenticity: Authenticates that the data comes from a verified source.

Types of Basic Encryption

Symmetric Encryption

Uses the same key for encryption and decryption. It's fast and suitable for large amounts of data.

Hash Functions

Converts data into a fixed size hash value, which cannot be reversed, ideal for password storage.

AES128

Pros

  • Fast encryption and decryption.
  • Requires less computational power.
  • Adequate for most conventional purposes.

Cons

  • Lower security margin compared to AES256.
  • Potentially vulnerable to future advances in quantum computing.

When to Use

Use AES128 when you require fast and efficient encryption for applications like streaming video content where performance is crucial.

AES192

Pros

  • More secure than AES128 due to a longer key length.
  • Still relatively efficient in terms of computational resources.

Cons

  • Slower than AES128 due to a longer key.
  • Not as widely adopted or supported as AES128 or AES256.

When to Use

AES192 might be used when there is a need for a balance between stronger security and computational efficiency, though it's less common than AES128 and AES256.

AES256

Pros

  • Highest security margin among the AES variants.
  • Theoretically resistant to all known practical attacks, including brute force.

Cons

  • Slower than AES128 due to the complexity of the key.
  • Requires more computational resources, which can be a drawback in resource-constrained environments.

When to Use

AES256 is best suited for scenarios that require the highest level of security, such as securing sensitive government or financial data, where encryption/decryption speed is not the primary concern.
